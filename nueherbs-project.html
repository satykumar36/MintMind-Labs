The Client

Neuherbs is a leading Indian health and wellness brand that has been empowering people to live healthier lives through natural nutrition since its inception. Offering a wide range of plant-based supplements, superfoods, vitamins, and wellness solutions, Neuherbs combines the power of nature and science to deliver high-quality, safe, and effective products.

The Challenge

Neuherbs initially came to us for help with website speed and performance. As a brand driven by trust, wellness, and vibrant product visuals, they were selling primarily to mobile shoppers, but their customers’ devices couldn’t keep up with Neuherbs’ growing product catalog and numerous health categories. When visitors can’t browse or shop smoothly, they don’t convert. And Neuherbs knew they were leaving valuable sales on the table.

How We Did It

To improve Neuherbs’ website performance, we began by identifying and removing all unnecessary plugins, scripts, and unused code that were slowing down mobile page loads. Next, we optimized all product images, banners, and other assets for faster rendering and implemented lazy loading to enhance browsing speed on mobile devices.

As a result, Neuherbs’ bounce rate dropped by nearly 20%, allowing customers to spend more time exploring products and offers. This improvement not only created a smoother shopping experience but also helped increase overall conversions and engagement.

To optimize the Neuherbs shopping experience for mobile, we analyzed data from user behavior and session recordings to redesign Neuherbs’ category and product detail pages (PDPs). Mobile customers were often overwhelmed by the large number of health categories, filters, and product variants, leading to long scroll sessions and lower conversions.

Using these insights as the foundation for our redesign, we enhanced the user experience (UX) by simplifying navigation and refining the filtering process. We reduced the number of filter options visible on mobile collection pages and reorganized product information for clarity. Session data confirmed that these improvements reduced decision fatigue and helped users find and purchase products faster.
