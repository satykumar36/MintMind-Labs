The Client

Homely is a modern furniture brand that has been offering stylish, high-quality home furnishings designed for comfort and durability. Specializing in sofas, beds, and premium furniture pieces, Homely blends elegant design with everyday functionality to create spaces that truly feel like home. With a strong online presence and a commitment to craftsmanship, Homely provides fast delivery, reliable customer support, and a seamless shopping experience. Their dedication to quality, affordability, and modern design has earned them a loyal customer base and a trusted name in the furniture industry.

The Challenge

Homely initially came to us for help with website speed and overall shopping performance. As a brand known for quality craftsmanship and modern furniture design, they were attracting a growing base of mobile shoppers, but their customers’ devices struggled to handle Homely’s expanding product catalog and high-resolution lifestyle images. When visitors can’t browse or shop smoothly, they don’t convert. And Homely realized they were losing valuable sales opportunities because of it.

How We Did It

To enhance Homely’s website performance, we began by identifying and removing all unnecessary apps, scripts, and outdated code that were slowing down page speed and affecting user experience. Next, we optimized all lifestyle images, banners, and visual assets for faster load times and implemented lazy loading to boost performance and responsiveness across devices.

As a result, Homely saw an impressive 20% drop in bounce rate, allowing visitors to browse more products and stay longer on the site. These improvements not only made the overall experience smoother and faster but also helped increase conversions and customer satisfaction.

To optimize the Homely shopping experience for mobile, we analyzed customer behavior and session recordings to redesign Homely’s category and product detail pages (PDPs). Mobile shoppers were often overwhelmed by the wide variety of furniture styles, color options, and material choices, which led to longer browsing sessions and fewer completed purchases.

Using these insights as the foundation for our redesign, we improved the user experience (UX) by simplifying navigation and refining the filtering process. We reduced the number of visible filter options on mobile category pages and presented product details more clearly. Session data showed that these updates minimized choice overload and helped customers find and purchase their desired furniture more quickly.
